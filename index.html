A Community-Based
Gunshot Alert System

Empowering communities with affordable, privacy-preserving technology to detect and report illegal gunfire

What We Do

The CivicAlert project links community engagement with advanced technology to address gun violence. By deploying a network of inexpensive, resident-hosted acoustic sensors, we provide a scalable, community-controlled solution for detecting and localizing illegal gunfire. In partnership with the North Austin Civic Association (NACA), this pilot empowers residents with real-time information, improving safety, fostering peace of mind, and enhancing the effectiveness of law enforcement response.

How It Works

1

Community Sensing

Residents host small, privacy-preserving acoustic sensors that plug into an outlet on the exterior of their home. These devices detect loud impulsive sounds like gunfire while ignoring normal street noise.

2

Intelligent Verification

Potential gunshots are securely analyzed using on-device AI to filter out sounds like fireworks and backfires. Data from multiple sensors are fused to pinpoint the exact location of the gunfire.

3

Instant Alerts

Qualified alerts are immediately sent to community members via text message, email, or mobile app, empowering residents with real-time safety information and conferring peace of mind.

4

Community Action

Residents can call or text the police with precise, actionable information, significantly improving the efficiency and effectiveness of the law enforcement response.

Mobile App-Based Monitoring and Alerts

Receive instant notifications when gunfire is detected in your neighborhood. The mobile app provides real-time information, allowing you to make informed decisions and enhancing community situational awareness.

Users may also manage their devices, view historical data about gunfire in their neighborhood, or quickly generate a script to help them effectively report gunfire to the police. They may also access these tasks through a web interface and receive alerts via email or text message.

Crowd-Sourced Localization

CivicAlert uses a distributed network of community-hosted sensors to triangulate the source of gunfire with high precision. By combining data from multiple devices, the system can distinguish between actual threats and false alarms, providing more reliable data to residents and first responders. Residents may also report detection inaccuracies through the mobile app to help improve the quality of alerts.

Stress-Free Community Reporting

When an alert is received, you have the option of generating a call script to help you quickly and efficiently communicate the right information to the 911 dispatcher. You may also auto-generate an SMS-based 911 alert. Verified law enforcement personnel will be able to access a record of the incident you are describing and link it to your call using a unique Incident ID for faster processing and cross-referencing of reports.

What Makes Us Different

  • Residents choose to participate voluntarily
  • No active audio surveillance or invasion of privacy
  • Community members receive alerts directly and promptly
  • You control your own device and see the same type of information as the police

Affordable & Accessible

  • Service is a fraction of the cost of commercial systems
  • No internet required, uses its own dedicated cellular network
  • Simple plug-and-play installation
  • Receive alerts via text, email, or app
  • Free for pilot participants

Collaborative Approach

  • Designed with input from residents, police, and city officials
  • Supports rather than replaces community reporting
  • Helps law enforcement respond more effectively
  • Builds trust through transparency
