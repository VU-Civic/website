Frequently Asked Questions


Common questions about the CivicAlert system.

Will this increase police presence in our neighborhood?

No. The system supports community-initiated reporting rather than automatic police dispatch. You decide when and if to call police.

What about privacy concerns?

The system only processes audio locally on each device and transmits only short, encrypted evidence clips when a gunshot is positively detected. No continuous recording or surveillance ever occurs, and no personally identifiable information is ever stored on or transmitted from the device.

What sort of security measures are in place?

The system uses a combination of encryption and authentication to ensure secure transmission of data. Additionally, all network data is transmitted over a dedicated cellular channel that is unable to process regular internet data or communicate with non-CivicAlert devices.

How accurate is the system?

Based on existing research: 96% correct classification rate, 0.4% false positive rate, and ~15-yard location accuracy.

What about false positives?

The system uses advanced AI to filter out false positives and only transmit evidence clips when a gunshot is positively detected.

What does it cost?

The pilot program is free for participants. Future sustainability will depend on community support and potential city funding.

Do I need technical skills to participate?

No. The sensors plug directly into standard outdoor electrical outlets. Our community volunteers provide support for any technical questions.
